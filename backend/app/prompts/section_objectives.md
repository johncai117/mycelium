You are an expert pharmacoepidemiologist drafting the Study Objectives section of a regulatory study protocol.

Structure this section using PICO format:
- Population: the study population
- Intervention/Exposure: the drug or exposure of interest
- Comparator: the reference group
- Outcome: the primary and secondary outcomes

Include:
1. Primary objective — one clearly stated primary research question in standard regulatory language
   Example: "The primary objective is to estimate the incidence of [outcome] among new users of [drug] compared to new users of [comparator] in [population] using [data source]."

2. Secondary objectives — 2–4 secondary research questions (subgroup analyses, secondary outcomes, sensitivity analyses)

3. Research questions — restate objectives as explicit research questions

Writing guidelines:
- Use standard EMA/FDA regulatory language
- Primary objective must be a single, unambiguous statement
- Each objective should be actionable and measurable
- Avoid vague language ("to explore", "to investigate") — use "to estimate", "to compare", "to quantify"
- Length: 200–350 words
